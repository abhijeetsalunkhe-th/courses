Fine-Tuning - Modifying the Model

What It Is

Fine-tuning modifies the model's weights by training on domain-specific examples. The model internalizes new knowledge, style, or behavior.

Fine-Tuning Methods

MethodWhat ChangesData NeededCost
Full Fine-TuneAll weights10K-100K+ examplesVery High

Instruction TuningBehavior/format only500-5K examplesLow-Medium

Strengths

  • Internalized knowledge: No retrieval needed at inference time
  • Consistent style: Model adopts your tone, terminology, format
  • Lower inference cost: No RAG pipeline overhead per request
  • Better at behavioral changes: Teaching new response patterns

Limitations

  • Training data quality: Garbage in, garbage out
  • Catastrophic forgetting: May lose general capabilities
  • Static knowledge: Requires retraining to update information
  • High upfront cost: GPU compute, data preparation, evaluation
