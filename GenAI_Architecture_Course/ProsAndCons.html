Architectural Pros and Cons

Every architectural decision in a GenAI system involves trade-offs. Understanding these pros and cons is essential for building sustainable AI applications.

Monolith Architecture

  • Pros: Simplest to build, deploy, and debug. Lowest latency for internal communication.
  • Cons: Hard to scale resource-intensive AI components independently. Single point of failure.

Event-Driven Architecture

  • Pros: Excellent for long-running tasks. Highly scalable and resilient. Decouples expensive AI calls from the user-facing API.
  • Cons: Increases system complexity. Requires managing message brokers and handling asynchronous state.

Serverless Architecture

  • Pros: Automatic scaling from zero. Zero infrastructure management. Pay only for exact execution time.
  • Cons: Cold starts can increase latency. Limited execution time and memory for very large AI pipelines.

Key Trade-offs in GenAI

  1. Latency vs. Cost: Premium models offer better results but at higher cost and latency. Caching reduces both but adds complexity.
  2. Complexity vs. Scalability: Event-driven systems scale better but are harder to maintain than monoliths.
  3. Privacy vs. Convenience: Self-hosting models (Local/VPC) offers maximum privacy but requires significant infrastructure effort compared to using cloud APIs.

Final Recommendations

  • Start with a Monolith for your MVP to validate the product-market fit quickly.
  • Move to Event-Driven once you have long-running tasks or need to scale background processing.
  • Implement Caching Early to save costs and improve user experience from day one.
