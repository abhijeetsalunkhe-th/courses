GenAI Application Architecture Patterns

Welcome to GenAI Application Architecture Patterns. Designing robust, scalable, and cost-effective GenAI applications requires a deep understanding of architectural trade-offs. This course covers the most effective patterns for building AI-powered systems, from simple monoliths to sophisticated event-driven and serverless architectures.

Learning Objectives

  • Evaluate different architecture patterns (Monolith, Microservices, Event-Driven, Serverless) for GenAI.
  • Implement asynchronous processing strategies to handle long-running LLM tasks.
  • Apply scaling techniques such as horizontal scaling, model tiering, and prompt caching.
  • Design for cost optimization by choosing the right models and caching strategies.
  • Analyze the pros and cons of various architectural decisions in real-world scenarios.

Target Audience

This course is designed for:

  • Software Architects designing next-generation AI systems.
  • Full-stack Developers building GenAI-integrated applications.
  • DevOps/SRE Engineers responsible for scaling and monitoring AI workloads.

Prerequisites

A solid foundation in software engineering and familiarity with API-based services is required. Knowledge of cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure/GCP) and containerization is highly beneficial.

Course Structure

  • Module 1: GenAI Architecture Patterns: Monolith, Event-Driven, and Serverless comparison.
  • Module 2: Async Processing and Scaling: Handling latency and optimizing for high-volume traffic.
  • Pros and Cons: A deep dive into the trade-offs of architectural decisions.
  • Final Assessment: A comprehensive test of your GenAI architecture knowledge.

You need to score 70% or higher on each assessment to proceed.
